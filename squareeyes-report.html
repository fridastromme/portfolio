Report: Square Eyes

When I started developing Square Eyes almost a year ago, I had no frontend experience. Since then, the site has been through several rounds of improvements.

I had a lot of fun designing Square Eyes in the design course at the beginning of the year. I didn't know the limitations at the time, or where I would meet challenges when developing the site. I a sense, this was a good thing, because it forced me to go into a steep learning curve, and I developed my skills quickly during the initial process of creating the site with HTML and CSS.

Improvements

I went back to the last submission of the site and looked at the feedback I got on the assignment. From there I used what I learned during the Exam 1 period, and created a modal for the infobox that pops up when you click the featured movie. The information here is also dynamically created through Wordpress REST API. This means that when updating the featured movie in Wordpress, the information modal will also be automatically updated, which is practical. The rest of the movies are also dynamically fetched from Wordpress' Woocommerce.

Meta titles and meta descriptions are also dynamically updated through the API depending on which movie you click on. This way, you don't need to hard code each of these every time you make a change in the lists of movies.

I used JavaScript to change the styling of the hover states when hovering over the movie image. Earlier, the styling of the movie titles would only change when hovering over the title itself. This is more pleasing and less confusing for the user.

On the purchase movie page, I added a shadow to the purchase information section in order to highlight this more. I also adapted the page better to smaller screens, and the layout changes from a two column grid to a one column grid depending on the screen width.

The user profile pages had a lot of static pixel widts defined on the different sections, which made these pages adapt poorly to different screen widths. I changed the widts to percentages and max-widths instead. This really improved how the different sections adapted to different screen sizes.

I also added some subtle animations to some of the elements on the site. Like a zoom effect when hovering over the different movie images to further indicate that these are clickable. These small movements are also meant to be more pleasing for the user, and are intended to make it more interesting to click these elements.

I tidied up the header and footer navigations by aligning the elements better. I also improved the alignment on some of the call to action buttons, both on the front page and on the user profiles. These also have an improved color contrast. The navigation on the sign up and sign in pages are also improved for assistive technology by adding aria-labels for easier navigation between the different sections.

The forms should also now be labeled correctly, with a for attribute pointing to the id of the input. This way, the user knows which input the label refers to.

During the different rounds of improving the Square Eyes site, I've both been adding and removing classes and ids. I took some time to clean up and remove the ones who weren't used anymore. The code is now simplified compared to earlier versions, which makes it easier to develop further and gives me a better overview.

Conclusion

This project is especially characterized by the fact that I learned the very basics of frontend development while creating the site from scratch. This means that several places I needed to spend some time understanding what I intented to do at the time. A portion of the improvements consisted of cleaning up the classes and simplifying the elements. I still think that further improvements could be made in this area. There might also be some places where small adjustments to margins and paddings would improve the design even further. At this point in time, I think Square Eyes has improved significantly and it is a testament to how much I've learned during one year.

Frida Strømme
